Water Cycle

How does water behave in its different states?

Water can exist in three states: solid, liquid, and gas. The three types of phase changes for water are:

  • Evaporation: When water is in a liquid state, it can evaporate into a gas or freeze into a solid.
  • Melting: If water is in a frozen state, then adding heat will melt it and turn it into a liquid again.
  • Condensation: When water vapor in the air condenses, it turns back into a liquid, such as in the form of rain.

All changes of state involve the transfer of energy. The water particles in each state behave as energy is absorbed or released.
